F1 Timeline

The Beginning

1950

Formula One World Championship begins at Silverstone, UK. Giuseppe Farina becomes the first World Champion driving an Alfa Romeo.

Constructor's Championship

1958

The Constructor's Championship is introduced, adding a new dimension to the sport. Vanwall wins the inaugural title.

Hunt vs Lauda

1976

One of F1's most dramatic seasons sees James Hunt and Niki Lauda battle it out, with Hunt winning by a single point.

McLaren Dominance

1988

McLaren-Honda wins 15 out of 16 races with Ayrton Senna and Alain Prost, one of the most dominant seasons ever.

Ferrari Renaissance

2000

Michael Schumacher brings Ferrari their first Drivers' Championship in 21 years, starting an era of dominance.

Hybrid Era Begins

2014

F1 introduces hybrid power units, combining turbo engines with energy recovery systems for a new technological era.

New Generation

2022

Revolutionary technical regulations bring ground-effect aerodynamics back to F1, promoting closer racing.
